These 5 Make-Ahead Breakfasts Are the Best Wake-Up Call

By Jen Harwell

  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • Google+
  • Pinterest
  • StumbleUpon

There are few things in life that'll will tempt you out of bed faster than the delicious aroma of breakfast in the morning. But, unless you’re checked into in a B&B, chances are you're the one making breakfast. Here's the perfect solution: Assemble a breakfast casserole the night before and bake it in the morning. We have tips for making the best overnight breakfast casseroles, plus 5 of our most popular recipes, perfect for busy weekdays, lazy weekends, or hosting overnight guests. Now you can rest easy knowing you’ve got breakfast handled.

Top Tips for Make-Ahead Breakfast Casseroles

  • If your recipes calls for bread, make sure it's day-old bread or dry it out a bit in a warm oven. This helps the bread absorb eggs, milk, or other liquid without making the casserole mushy.
  • For breakfast casseroles, cook onions, mushrooms, or bacon fully before layering them up with the other ingredients, otherwise they might come out tasting a bit raw.
  • Defrost and drain frozen vegetables before adding them to your casserole. Squeeze defrosted spinach until scant moisture remains.
  • Use a glass or ceramic baking dish when you're making breakfast casseroles because eggs can react with metal pans causing discoloration or odd flavors.
  • Coat the inside of your baking dish to make serving and clean-up that much easier.
  • Use wide, shallow baking pans if you want more surface area for crunchy toppings, but you might have to cut back the total baking time a few minutes. Just keep an eye on it and you'll be fine.
  • Fill your baking dish to about three-quarters full to allow room for ingredients to expand or bubble up while baking.

5 Amazing Overnight Breakfast Casserole Recipes

1. Overnight Blueberry French Toast | Bakes in 1 hour

Want to seriously impress your overnight guests? Here's the recipe everyone loves. Rich, sweet, creamy, and served with a tangy blueberry sauce over the top or on the side. Watch the video to see how to make it.

2. Farmer’s Casserole | Bakes in 45 minutes

Layer your favorite vegetables in this hash brown and egg casserole. Reviewers suggest spicing it up with pepperjack cheese, canned chilies, or jalapenos.

Farmer's Casserole | Photo by Allrecipes Magazine

3. Breakfast Strata | Bakes in 1 hour 15 minutes

Feed a crowd with this big beautiful strata. But, it’s easily scaled if you’re just cooking for two, just click on “Change Servings” to change up the recipe.

Breakfast Strata | Photo by hello angie

4. Baked Coconut French Toast with Pineapple-Rum Sauce | Bakes in 30 minutes

The recipe titles tells you everything you need to know about this tropical breakfast treat. Adding rum to the pineapple sauce is, of course, strictly optional. *wink*

Baked Coconut French Toast with Pineapple-Rum Sauce | Photo by Allrecipes Magazine

5. Eggs Benedict Casserole | Bakes in 50 minutes

Eggs Benedict is easier when you make it ahead of time. Use Canadian bacon, ham, English muffins and hollandaise sauce to make this tangy breakfast casserole.
